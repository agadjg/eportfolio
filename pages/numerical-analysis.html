Why is the p-value not enough?

Reporting statistics truthfully and transparently, while acknowledging uncertainty in decision-making, is neither simple nor straightforward. Although the p-value indicates statistical significance, it does not convey the magnitude, precision, or practical significance of an observed effect. The key aspects are reflected by the confidence interval. Adopting a holistic approach is essential to minimise misinterpretation and support effective clinical decision-making.
